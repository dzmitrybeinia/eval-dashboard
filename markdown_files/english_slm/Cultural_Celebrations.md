# Lesson name: Exploring Global Cultural Celebrations

## Question 1: YesNo

**Statement:** During Diwali, people decorate their homes with colorful designs.

**Answer:** True

**Feedback:**
Rangoli, which are vibrant patterns created on the ground or the floor, are indeed an integral part of Diwali decorations. These designs are not only visually appealing but also carry cultural significance, often reflecting themes related to the festival.


## Question 2: YesNo

**Statement:** In Hanukkah, the menorah is lit for nine nights to symbolize the miracle of the oil.

**Answer:** False

**Feedback:**
The menorah is traditionally lit for eight nights during Hanukkah, not nine, to commemorate the miracle of the oil that lasted for the same duration.


## Question 3: YesNo

**Statement:** During Día de los Muertos, people typically decorate graves with wilted flowers.

**Answer:** False

**Feedback:**
Graves are not decorated with wilted flowers. Instead, vibrant marigolds, known as 'cempasúchil,' are used to guide the spirits back to their families.


## Question 4: YesNo

**Statement:** During Día de los Muertos, people decorate altars to honor their ancestors.

**Answer:** True

**Feedback:**
The celebration you're referring to involves families preparing special altars and offering food, flowers, and mementos to their loved ones who have passed away. This practice is a significant aspect of the festival, reflecting the community's respect and veneration for those who are no longer with us.
